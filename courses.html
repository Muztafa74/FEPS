Electronic government systems

Dr. Shrief Abdelrahman

Introduction

The Course introduces students to a range of e-government concepts, topics, Techniques, methodologies and readings. Lay the foundations of e-government as an emerging interdisciplinary area of research and practice and stress the potential offered by ICT for a greater interactivity between the citizen and the government The target of this course is to lay the foundations of e-government and e-politics as an emerging interdisciplinary area of research and practice, and stressing the potential offered by the Internet for a greater interactivity between the public and political spheres.

Outcomes

Upon completion, the student should be able to: explain how the Internet is disrupting old political behaviors and enabling innovative new approaches to governance and advocacy, identify the changing rules of the political game, and explain how the Web is changing traditional politics, define Internet impact on state-citizen relationship, analyze different types of digital representation and identify “two-way” forms of communication.

Frequently Questions

Are there any prequisite courses in the minor required before enrolling in E-Government course?

No, you just have to be in a ssc minor

Is there a section for the course?

Yes, There is a section for the students of the course to apply with the TA the theoretical part of the course by coding using HTML, CSs.

What's the Topics covered in the course?

The course cover a wide scope of studies regarding E-government like Internet Freedom and its Discontents, The Politics of the Internet, Digital Citizenship,Virtual Communities,Digital Democracy, E-participation, On-line advocacy, Digital parliaments

In what language is the course taught?

The course is taught mainly in English as the main materials of the course is all English. However the professor may present in Arabic to facilitate understanding
